# Pegasus & PRISM: Surveillance in the Digital Age
### Open Research Reports — Ratnesh Raj Dwivedi | 2026

Two comprehensive research reports examining the most powerful surveillance systems of the modern era — NSO Group's Pegasus spyware and the NSA's PRISM intelligence program.

---

## PRISM Report — Key Findings
- PRISM operated under Section 702 of FISA, enabling NSA to collect data from Google, Facebook, Apple, Microsoft, and Yahoo without individual warrants
- Edward Snowden's 2013 disclosures exposed a surveillance apparatus that had outpaced every legal and democratic oversight mechanism in place
- The program created a permanent "chilling effect" — journalists, activists, and citizens changed their digital behaviour after learning they had been watched

## Pegasus Report — Key Findings
- Pegasus can infect both iPhone and Android devices with zero user interaction — no tap, no click, no download required
- Deployed in 45+ countries; confirmed targets include journalists, judges, opposition politicians, and children of surveillance targets
- NSO Group faces legal damages exceeding $2 billion; the company lost 40%+ of its workforce after US Entity List designation in 2021
- Mexico spent $80M+ on Pegasus — used not against cartels, but against journalists and their families
